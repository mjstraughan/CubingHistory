As I assume David knows, a 5-cycle can be broken down into two 3- cycles. So let's say you put numbered stickers on the 5 pieces that you want to cycle so that 1->2->3->4->5->1 (meaning piece #1 needs to go where piece #2 is, etc.) or also written (1,2,3,4,5). You can pick any of 5 possible cycles as your first cycle: (1,2,3), (2,3,4), (3,4,5), (4,5,1), or (5,1,2). Let's say you pick (2,3,4) because there is a nice insertion for that with a good number of cancellations. You then will also need to either: pick an insertion for (4,5,1) earlier than the (2,3,4) insertion point, or pick an insertion for (5,1,2) later than the (2,3,4) insertion point. That is, if the earlier insertion is for (n,n+1,n+2), the later insertion should be for (n+3,n+4, n). (Of course, modulo arithmetic is assumed here so that n+4 would mean n-1 if the n+4 is literally > 5.) The above assumes you go through the solve looking for all possible insertions without yet actually performing any of the insertions found. You can then try to pick a pair of insertions matching the above constraints that minimize the number of added moves (taking cancellations into account). This assumes the insertion points you pick aren't too close together that one insertion point gets cancelled out by the other. Also, as Mirek pointed out, this will not take into consideration the possibility of making an insertion within an insertion, so you may not end up with best possible choice. But with limited time, you may not have time to explore all possibilities anyway. You will have to choose how to proceed based upon what you estimate will give you the best chances to find as good as solution as possible in the time you have. - Bruce --- In fewestmoveschallenge@yahoogroups.com, "mirek_goljan" <goljan@...> wrote: > > --- In fewestmoveschallenge@yahoogroups.com, "david_thielen2002" > <mthielen68@> wrote: > > > > --- In fewestmoveschallenge@yahoogroups.com, "david_thielen2002" > > <mthielen68@> wrote: > > > > > > I'm practicing multiple insertions within an alotted amount of > time. > > In > > > FMC 205 I had a five corner cycle.
